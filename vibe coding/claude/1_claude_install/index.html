Chapter I

The spaces we carry

We spend our lives moving through spaces — rooms, streets, landscapes — but rarely do we allow those spaces to move through us.

The hands that shaped these walls, the light that finds these floors daily — this is the quiet language of permanence. To dwell is to leave a trace upon the surface of the world.

What remains when we are gone is not the object, but the memory of light upon it.
